**8. Critical Reflection**

In this homework, we trained a Logistic Regression model on the BreastMNIST dataset to establish a classical machine learning baseline. The model achieved a test accuracy of approximately 76%, showing that it can learn to differentiate between benign and malignant ultrasound images. Generalization in this toy setting means that the model successfully applies the core patterns it learned from the training data to new, unseen patient images. However, because the dataset is relatively small, the results might be skewed by a very small test set, creating an unstable metric. Another major risk is overfitting to noise, where the algorithm memorizes irrelevant pixel artifacts instead of the actual biological tumor shapes. If this happens, the model will fail to generalize when used on images from a different hospital. Our results visually confirm the limitations of this baseline. As seen in our 2D PCA decision boundary plot, a simple straight line cannot perfectly separate the complex, non-linear overlap between the two classes. Therefore, while this PyTorch implementation successfully fulfills the baseline objective, it highlights why real medical imaging requires more complex, non-linear architectures to ensure true generalization as Feed Forward Neural Networks that were discussed on the book.
